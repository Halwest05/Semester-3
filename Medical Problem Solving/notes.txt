Edema and Cyanosis:
	Edema:
		Causes:
			1- Increased total extracellular fluid
			2- Increased oncotic pressure & serum albumin
			3- High local venous pressure
			4- High permeability of capilliaries
			5- Lymphatic obstruction

		Pitting edema (mainly peripheral edema): Indentation when pressure is applied and persists.
	
		Non-Pitting edema: Indentation does not occur, its typical of lymphatic obstruction, results of excessive matrix deposition. ex/ Hypothyroidism
	
		Investigations:
			1- Examination of cvs and abdomen.
			2- Imaging of organs. (Liver, kidney, heart)
			3- blood tests (Electrolytes, serum albumin)
			4- urine test (protein)

	Cyanosis:
		definition: Bluish color of skin, result of increased reduced HB (Deoxygenated HB).

		2 types:
			1- Central Cyanosis
			2- Peripheral Cyanosis
	
		Causes of central cyanosis:
			1- Decreased atmospheric pressure
			2- Impaired pulmonary function
			3- Alveolar hypoventilation
			4- Inhomogenity in pulmonary ventilation
			5- Decreased arterial oxygen saturation
			6- Impaired oxygen diffusion
			7- Hemoglobin with low affinity for oxygen
			8- Hemoglobin abnormality
			9- Congenital heart disease

		Causes of peripheral cyanosis:
			1- Reduced cardiac output
			2- cold exposure
			3- Redistribution of blood flow from extremities
			4- Arterial obstruction
			5- Venous obstruction

		Features important to detecting the cause of cyanosis:
			1- ascertain the time on onset.
			2- type of cyanosis. (Central or peripheral)
			3- presence of clubbing of the digits should be ascertained.
			4- Pa02 and Sa02 should be determined

Vitamin D:
	* Main function is the regulation of calcium absorption and homeostasis.

	* %50 is absorbed of dietary.
	* Dietary vitamins absorbed in intestines.
	* Low levels of vitamin D cause iron defficiency
	
	2 types:
		1- V-D3 (Cholecalciferol)
		2- V-D2 (Ergocalciferol)

	* Transported by DBP (Vitamin D binding protein)

	Dehydrocholestrol -> hydrocholestrol -> cholecalciferol

	Funcs:
		Principal func: Maintain plasma level of calcium ions.
		
		1- Increased intestinal absorption of calcium.
		2- Reduced excretion of calcium ion by kidney.
		3- Mobilization of bone mineral
		4- Secretion and synthesis of insulin, thyroid and parathyroid hormones.
		5- Inhibition of production of interleukin.
		6- modulation of cell diffrentiation, proliferation, apoptosis

	Rickets:
		Def: undermineralization of bone in babies.
		
		Features:
			1- Rachitic rosary
			2- Frontal bossing
			3- Wide sutures
			4- Dental hypoplasia
			5- bowing of legs
			6- Swelling of hands and ankle joint

	Osteomalacia: Demineralization of bone in adults.

Iron Defficiency:
	iron containing heme-proteins:
		1- Cytochrome of mitochondria
		2- Hemoglobin
		3- Myoglobin
		4- Cytochrome p-450 of liver

	iron containing non-heme-proteins:
		1- Ferritin (stores iron)
		2- Transferrin (Transfers iron)

	* Iron in men: (20-275 micro-gram/L)
	* Iron in women: (5-200 micro-gram/L)
	
	* Less than 15 micro-gram/L causes iron def

	Disorders:
		1- Hemochromatosis -> Increased absorption of iron
		2- Iron def anemia -> Decreased amount of iron
		3- Anemia of chronic disease -> inflammation induced change of iron distrobution

	Causes:
		1- Chronic blood loss	
		2- Increased demand of iron
		3- Malabsorption of iron
		4- Inadequate iron intake
		5- Vascular hemolysis

	Lab features:
		Hb, Htc, Rbc: Low
		Serum iron: Low
		Serum Ferritin: Low
		Transferrin saturation: Low

		Retics: Normal, Low
		PLT: normal,high,low
		wbc: Normal, low

		TIBC (Totial iron binding capacity): High
		Serum transferrin receptor: High
		FEP, RDW: HIGH

	Cells able to export iron:
		1- Macrophages
		2- Enterocytes
		3- Hepatocytes

	Hepcidin:
		* Blocks iron export, released from liver according to 3 states:
			1- Iron overload -> increase
			2- Iron defficency -> decrease
			3- inflammation -> increase

Jaundice:
	* caused by hyperbilirubinemia
	* %60 infants, %80 pre-infants
	
	2 types of hyperbilirubinemia:
		1- Indirect (Unconjugated) not bound to albumin -> Potentially neurotoxic
			* Skin appears bright yellow, orange

		2- Direct (conjugated) bound to albumin -> Signfies a serious illness
			* Skin appears greenish
		
	Etiology:
		Fetal: placenta is main route of elimination of bilirubin.
		Adulthood: water-soluble conjugated form of bilirubin excreted like this:
			hepatic cell -> bile -> billary system -> GIT

		Unconjugated hyperbillirubinemia causes:
			1- increase load of billrubin
			2- Reduce activity of transferase
			3- blocking of transferase
			4- abscence or reduction in amount of transferase/ billirubin absorbed by liver

		Toxicity of unconjugated billirubinemia increased by:
			1- reduction of excretion of billirubin in cirulation
			2- increase of permeability of the blood brain barrier

		* Jaundice appearing at 2nd or 3rd day are usually physiologic.
		* Jaundic appearing after 3rd day and within 1st week suggest Bacterial sepsis, UTI

		Risk factors of indirect hyperbillirubinemia:
			1- maternal diabetes
			2- prematurity
			3- drugs
			4- polycythemia
			5- male sex
			6- 21-trisomy (Down syndromic)

Syncope:
	Def: Transient or episodic loss of consciousness due to reduced cerebral perfusion.

	Types:
		1- CS disease: arrhythmia
		2- Neuro-cardiogenic cause: (abnormal autonomic reflex), vasovagal syncope
		3- Orthostatic hypotension: bp drop on changing posture
		4- Neurological or psychiatric diseases:
			1- Ischemic attacks
			2- panic attacks
			3- hyperventilation

Fall:
	Symptoms: Fracture, brusing, pain, bleeding
	
	Complications: Fractures, injuries, fear of falling, death, potential immobilization

	Physical risk factors:
		1- blood pressure regulation
		2- Gait disturbance
		3- CNS problems
		4- vision
		5- otolaryngologic functions
		6- proprioception
	
	Behavioral risk factors:
		1- Medications
		2- lack of excercise
		3- Staying indoors

	Enviromental risk factors:
		1- Slippery surfaces
		2- hazards in home
		3- improper footwear
		4- incorrect use of walking aids
	
	Evaluating a fall:
		1- Clinical evaluation
		2- Tests
		3- Lab tests

Vital signs:

	2- Pulse
		rate < 60 => bradycardia

	3- Respiration
		rate < 12 => bradypnea
		rate > 16 => tachypnea
